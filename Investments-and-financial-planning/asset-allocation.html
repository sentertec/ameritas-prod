Asset allocation is a process to divide your portfolio among the major asset classes of bonds, equities, alternative investments, and cash or cash equivalents. Each asset class historically has different levels of risk and return which behave differently over time. By diversifying your money across these asset classes, you may lower your portfolio's overall risk exposure while maintaining your return objectives.
