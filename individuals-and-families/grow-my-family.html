grow my family

Adding a new child to your family is definitely one of life’s most exciting events. Whether you are having a baby or adopting a child, this is truly a joyous time.

Of course, growing your family also means your daily expenses can increase. While diapers and formula may be front and center, it’s also very important to know what to watch for in your daily expenses. Creating a budget will help you focus on your family’s financial planning needs.

Here’s a few things to keep in mind:

  • Add your child(ren) to your existing health insurance plan.
  • Increase flexible spending account amounts.
  • Review the number of allowances on your W-4 tax form.

Also you may want to:

  • create or revise your own will
  • apply for a Social Security Number for your child(ren)

Right now, your life is probably a flurry of activity with family and friends calling and stopping by to meet the newest member of your family. A new child brings joy and bliss to your life but also brings a new sense of urgency to financial planning. Some questions to consider:

  • Do you have enough life insurance now that a child depends on your income?
  • Is your disability insurance sufficient?
  • Would your child be able to attend college if something happened to you?

Family financial planning may derailed by the unexpected death of you or your partner. A disability or illness could prevent you or your partner from working and side track your own retirement plan. You may need to expand your family financial planning to include increases in life insurance and disability insurance to help ensure financial security for your child.
