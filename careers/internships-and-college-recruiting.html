And we strive to bring the future to us.  We believe in providing learning opportunities to introduce talented individuals to our businesses and culture.  Internships are an essential element of our future.  They provide the opportunity for us to recruit and develop the best candidates to support our mission, vision and values.

Benefits of an Internship

Job Fit

as you decide that you want to pursue a career in a particular field, you won't know until you actually try the job. Internships give you the real life experience that can reinforce the passion you believe you have, or it will help avoid years of training for the wrong career area.

Essential Experience

 having an internship on your resume can improve the likelihood of an interview. Employers like to see that you have some hands on experience. Even if it is a short term opportunity, its a critical piece of succeeding in your chosen career field.

Networking

the greatest resource we have are the experienced professionals that are willing to share their knowledge with the next generation. Some information simply can't be learned from a book.

College Credit or Certification

many programs offer college credit or program certification for internships.

Post Graduation Employment

"Employers say they extended job offers to nearly 70 percent of their interns last year...", Marilyn Mackes, National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE).

College Recruitment

Ameritas supports our local high schools, colleges and universities by participating in career fairs, mock interviewing, onsite interviews, job shadowing, speaking opportunities and maintaining ongoing, established partnerships with Career Centers.

How Internships Work at Ameritas

Ameritas offers different types of internships, depending on the needs of the different lines of business. Our goal is to provide varying avenues of learning opportunities, not just for interns, but, the potential of identifying future associates.  Ameritas opportunities include:

Rotational Internship

Our rotational internships are generally 18 - 24 months in length and focus on students going into their junior year of college.  These internships will focus on a specific job function within different lines of business (finance, legal, marketing, etc.). With exposure to different areas, the program prepares students for increasing responsibility through a combination of rotational assignments, structured learning, formalized feedback and mentoring. Interns have the benefit of interaction with senior leaders and participate in various key initiatives in different areas.

Summer Internship

Unique, short term opportunities to reinforce your career direction.

Project Internship

Internships can provide the foundation for a future career with the organization, especially as projects evolve and become important.  Various projects throughout the organization, specifically in our areas of Shared Services (accounting, project management, information technology, human resources, etc.) provide students with specific, career focused experience to support the creation, implementation and establishment of ongoing projects and objectives.

Why an internship?

Along with the benefit of an internship to a student, our managers are enthusiastic about sharing their knowledge with future leaders:

Kasey, Manager Organizational Development, IT - "Interns create a valuable partnership in which Ameritas learns and grows from the interns we hire. In the IT department, our interns are an essential ingredient to our mix. They add new ideas, energy and innovation that will help us reach our goals."

Lynn, Director Individual Product Support, Actuary - "The various actuarial departments utilize interns to bring in technical skills for a day to day impact on the projects we are involved with. We offer hands on experiences to those interested in actuarial science. This gives both the intern and the company a more in depth exposure to each other."

Laurie, Manager Shared Services, IT - "Interns bring a fresh perspective to the workplace. They ask why we do something a certain way.  We may have 'always done it that way' and no one questions it over time because it becomes a habit or ingrained process.  Interns come with skills and curiosity that compliment the skills and experience of our associates."
